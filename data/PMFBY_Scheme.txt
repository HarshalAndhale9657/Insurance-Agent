Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) - Crop Insurance Scheme
Trusted Source: Government of India

1. What is PMFBY?
PMFBY is the central government's crop insurance scheme to support farmers suffering crop loss/damage arising from non-preventable natural risks.

2. Risks Covered:
- Prevented Sowing/Planting Risk: Insured area is prevented from sowing/planting due to deficit rainfall or adverse seasonal conditions.
- Standing Crop (Sowing to Harvesting): Comprehensive risk insurance is provided to cover yield losses due to non-preventable risks, such as Dry spells, Drought, Flood, Inundation, Pests and diseases, Landslides, Natural Fire and Lightening, Storm, Hailstorm, Cyclone, Typhoon, Tempest, Hurricane and Tornado.
- Post-Harvest Losses: Coverage is available only up to a maximum period of 14 days from harvesting for those crops which are kept in "cut and spread" condition to dry in the field and get damaged due to cyclone and cyclonic rains and unseasonal rains.
- Localized Calamities: Loss/damage resulting from occurrence of identified localized risks of hailstorm, landslide, and Inundation affecting isolated farms in the notified area.

3. Premium Rates (Farmer's Share):
- Kharif Crops (e.g., Rice, Maize, Groundnut): 2% of Sum Insured or Actuarial Rate, whichever is less.
- Rabi Crops (e.g., Wheat, Barley, Mustard): 1.5% of Sum Insured or Actuarial Rate, whichever is less.
- Annual Commercial / Horticultural Crops: 5% of Sum Insured or Actuarial Rate, whichever is less.

The balance premium will be paid by the Government to provide full insured amount to the farmers.

4. How to Claim?
- Farmers must inform the insurance company or bank or local agriculture department within 72 hours of the calamity.
- App-based reporting via "Crop Insurance App" is encouraged.

5. Eligibility:
- All farmers growing notified crops in a notified area during the season who have insurable interest in the crop are eligible.
- Voluntary for non-loanee farmers. Mandatory for loanee farmers (those who have taken Kisan Credit Card loan).
